YMCA Trophy Case Design

The YMCA Trophy Case Design project showcases the achievements of the YMCA community through a modern, functional display. The design emphasizes clean lines and durable materials to create a versatile space for awards and memorabilia, enhancing the visual appeal of the facility.

The trophy case acts as a centerpiece that fosters a sense of pride and unity within the YMCA community. It is designed for adaptability and longevity, making it a lasting testament to member achievements.

Locker Project Overview: Site Visits, 3D Scans, and SketchUp Conversion

For this project, I began by conducting site visits to gather accurate data and understand the spatial requirements for the YMCA Trophy Case. During these visits, I used a LiDAR scanner to create detailed 3D scans of the location, which provided the foundation for the design.

Once I had the LiDAR scans, I imported the data into SketchUp for conversion into a 3D model. Here is the detailed process:

  1. Site Visit & Data Collection: Conducted site visits and used a handheld LiDAR scanner to capture the dimensions and spatial layout of the area designated for the trophy case.
  2. Data Processing: Imported the LiDAR scans into SketchUp to create an accurate base model of the space, ensuring every element matched the physical environment.
  3. 3D Model Conversion: Cleaned up the scan data to remove any extraneous details and focused on converting key elements into usable geometry within SketchUp.
  4. Design Development: Used the cleaned-up SketchUp model to develop the trophy case design, experimenting with different materials and configurations while making sure the design would fit seamlessly within the space.
  5. Visualization: Rendered the final model to create visualizations, which helped communicate the design intent clearly to stakeholders and ensure alignment with YMCA's aesthetic values.
